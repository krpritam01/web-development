6. List any 5 semantic tags in HTML along with thier descriptions.

Ans: Here are some of the commonly used semantic tags in HTML along with thier descriptions.

1. <header>: The <header> element represents the introductory content or a container for a group of introductory content at the beginning of a section, article, or page. It typically contains the site or section title, logo, navigation menu, or other relevant information.

2. <footer> - Represents the footer or bottom section of a web page, typically containing copyright information, contact details, and links to relevant pages or resources.

3. <nav> - Defines a section of the page that contains navigation links, such as menus and lists of links to other pages or parts of the same page.

4.<main>: The <main> element represents the main content of the document or the central content area of a web page.and should not contain any content that is repeated across multiple pages.

5. <article> - Defines a self-contained section of content on a web page, such as a blog post, news article, or product review.

These  tags provide meaningful structure and context to the content, making it easier for search engines, assistive technologies, and developers to understand and navigate the document. Using these tags appropriately enhances the accessibility, maintainability, and clarity of your HTML code.
